Chat Vault reads your messages file right here in your browser.

When you drop a file in, your browser reads it using built-in tools — the same way it opens a photo or PDF. Your messages are processed on your computer and displayed on your screen. That's it.

There's no server, no account, no upload. If you close this tab, everything is gone. We never see your data — it never leaves your machine.

Technical details

chat.db is read using SQL.js, a local database reader that runs in your browser via WebAssembly. You can verify there are no network requests in your browser's developer tools (Network tab).

This is normal — macOS is protecting your messages

Apple keeps your Messages folder locked down by default. This is a good thing — it means other apps can't read your conversations without your permission. To let Finder access it, you just need to flip one switch:

  1. Open System Settings (Apple menu → System Settings)
  2. Go to Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access
  3. Click the + button and add Finder
  4. You may need to unlock settings with your password first
  5. Close and reopen Finder, then try Step 1 again
What does Full Disk Access actually do? It allows Finder (your file browser) to see protected folders — that's all. It doesn't send anything online or give anyone else access. You're just telling your Mac "yes, I want to open my own files." You can remove Finder from this list anytime after you're done.

Alternative: add your browser instead

You can add Safari or Chrome to Full Disk Access instead of Finder. Then use the "click to browse" option in Step 3 to navigate directly to the file.

File missing or very small?

If chat.db doesn't exist or is under 1 MB, your messages may be stored in iCloud rather than on this Mac. This is most common on newer Macs that sync messages via iCloud.
