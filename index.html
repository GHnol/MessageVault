Get your Mac Messages
Follow these 3 steps — no Terminal needed
1

Open "Go to Folder" in Finder

First, click on your desktop or open a Finder window. Then press this keyboard shortcut:

⌘ ⇧ G

A small dialog box will appear at the top of the Finder window asking you where to go.

Go to Folder
~/Library/Messages/
2

Paste this path and press Enter

Copy the path below, paste it into the "Go to Folder" box, and press Enter.

~/Library/Messages/

Finder will open the Messages folder. You'll see a file called chat.db — that's the one you need.

3

Drag chat.db here

If the folder won't open or appears empty:

macOS protects your Messages data. You may need to grant Finder access:

  1. Open System Settings (click the Apple menu → System Settings)
  2. Go to Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access
  3. Click the + button
  4. Navigate to Applications → Finder and add it (or search for "Finder")
  5. You may need to unlock with your password first
  6. Close Finder and reopen it, then try Step 1 again

If you're using Safari or Chrome to access Chat Vault:

You can also add your browser to Full Disk Access. Same steps as above, but add Safari or Google Chrome instead of Finder. Then use the "click to browse" button in Step 3 above to navigate to the file.

Still not working?

On some Macs, Messages data may be stored only in iCloud and not locally. If chat.db is missing or very small (<1 MB), your messages may not be stored on this Mac.
